# UPDATED ON April 17, 2026
I followed the solution posted for this project and discovered that I was missing information on how the pulse function works for Timer 1 within the CubeMX application. From my understanding, the period needed for this Ultrasonic Sensor is around 50 us. Out of that period, there needs to be a 10us pulse (HIGH), leaving 40us LOW. By assigning the pulse to be 40 - 1, this accounts for the LOW section of 40 us needed before the 10us HIGH pulse. So Timer 1 was used for the trigger function, getting to 1us trigger using the prescalar of 167 and a counter period that sets the period to 50 us. Timer 2 was used for the echo, assigning Channel 1 to "Input capture direct mode" to capture both edges, rising and falling. This is also attached to an interrupt, which uses a flag to invoke the interrupt. The ISR compares the captured values of the rising and falling edge. If the falling edge is greater than the rising edge, math is performed to output the difference between the two and then divided by 58 to convert into cm. The flag is then reset at the end of this ISR.
